Vocabulary Builder: 11 Core Concepts

Explore 11 core concepts and professional verbs, categorized to enhance your vocabulary. Click on any card to reveal its definition and contextual examples.

#1 DISPUTE

MeaningExample
To engage in an argument or debate. (Squabble)Children normally get into small disputes with each other at school.
To argue or quarrel. (Quarrel)The two men were in a heated dispute.
To debate or discuss something. (Disagreement)The dispute over federal taxes was not resolved.

#2 DISSECT

MeaningExample
To cut apart. (Slice)We had to dissect the frog in science class.
To examine the structure (figuratively). (Divide)The man tried to dissect her answer but he couldn’t figure it out.
To examine in detail, part by part. (Break up)The doctor had to dissect the woman’s body in order to see what caused her death.

#3 SUBSTITUTE

MeaningExample
To take the place of (as a representative).Are we going to have a sub for our class tomorrow?
A person or thing acting in place of another.She was the best substitute teacher ever.

#4 CRAM

MeaningExample
To fill by force with more than it can easily hold. (Squeeze)The high school student tried to cram the night before her exam.
To force or stuff. (Compact)The man tried to cram all of the boxes into the small bag but it didn’t work.
To fill with an excessive amount (of work/information). (Pack)I’m trying to cram all of this work into one hour.

#5 GETAWAY

MeaningExample
An escape or quick departure, especially after a crime.The criminal was trying to make a getaway, but the cops caught him.
A short vacation or holiday.My friends and I are planning a weekend getaway to the beach.

#6 REBOUND

MeaningExample
To bounce back off something after hitting it.The small ball rebounded off the wall.
To increase or improve after a decline (business/health).The company had a bad year, but they were able to rebound quickly.

#7 ALL YOU CAN EAT

MeaningExample
Unlimited food for a fixed price.This is an all-you-can-eat buffet.
A fixed-price meal where quantity is unlimited.Our boss agreed to take us to an all-you-can-eat buffet for lunch.

#8 INFATUATION

MeaningExample
Extremely interested in someone/something for a short time.Other than an infatuation with Josh, there had been no one else.
A foolish or extravagant love/admiration.His infatuation with Sally made him go crazy.

#9 QUAINT

MeaningExample
Attractively unusual or old-fashioned.This is a quaint old house.
Strange or peculiar in a pleasing way.He has a quaint sense of humor.

#10 BLURRED

MeaningExample
Unable to see or be seen clearly (Hazy).Her blurred vision made her realize that she needed glasses.
Not clear or distinct.Everything blurred as she ran.

#11 ELUSIVE

MeaningExample
Difficult to find, catch, or achieve.Success will become more elusive in the next few years.
Hard to understand or remember (conceptually).The answers to the questions of life remain elusive.
